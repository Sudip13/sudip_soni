EV Battery Pack Assembly: Technical SOP Visualization

Role3D Visualization Specialist & Technical Content Developer
TechBlender, Adobe After Effects, Character Animation, Technical Scripting, Voiceover Generation

Description

This large-scale project involved the end-to-end development of over 50+ technical SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) videos for an electric vehicle battery pack production line. The objective was to create a visual encyclopedia of the assembly process, covering every station from initial component input to final quality checks.

My work focused on transforming complex engineering data into clear, instructional animations. I developed complete 3D models of industrial machinery and built the entire assembly line environment in Blender. I was responsible for analyzing technical SOPs—including inputs, outputs, processes, pre-requisites, and safety cautions—to script and animate accurate scenes involving both machine sequences and character-based human interactions. Depending on the workflow, I either managed the full production (generating voiceovers and final edits in After Effects) or collaborated with a post-production team by providing high-quality animation passes.

Outcome

Massive Training Library: Successfully delivered 50+ high-quality SOP videos, creating a comprehensive digital training resource for EV battery manufacturing.

Complex Process Breakdown: Simplified high-stakes assembly steps, ensuring that "Caution" and "Pre-requisite" notes were visually prioritized to reduce on-site errors and safety hazards.

Hybrid Production Workflow: Demonstrated versatility by handling both standalone end-to-end video creation and collaborative pipelines with After Effects editors.

High-Fidelity Industrial Simulation: Combined mechanical animation with character-based movements to provide a realistic "human-in-the-loop" perspective of the manufacturing floor.
